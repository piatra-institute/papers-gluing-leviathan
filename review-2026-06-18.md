# Review: gluing-leviathan

## Verdict
**Reject, or accept only with major revisions.** The simulation is clean and every headline decimal is faithfully reproduced by the code, the prose is genuinely alive, and the citations are real and (almost all) correctly used. But the paper's central scientific claim — that the first cohomology $H^1$ of the institutional sheaf "measures the obstruction to the order existing globally" — is not borne out by the paper's own numbers, and the cohomological machinery does no work that elementary set-intersection and brute-force enumeration don't already do. The single most serious problem: in the paper's own table, $H^1=1$ both for configurations with **512** global sections and for configurations with **0**, so $H^1$ demonstrably fails to detect the quantity the paper says it measures — and the one phenomenon that would justify the sheaf apparatus (a global obstruction with all pairwise overlaps non-empty) is never exhibited.

## Claim grounding

- **"The first cohomology $H^{1}$ of the sheaf measures the obstruction to the order existing globally."** (Abstract; restated §2.3) → **unsupported / contradicted by own data.** In the §4 table, `water_compact` alone has $H^1=1$ and **0** global sections; `water_compact + exchange_table` has $H^1=1$ and **512** global sections. Same $H^1$, opposite existence. $H^1$ does not separate "order exists" from "order impossible." The quantity that does — the integer global-section count — is computed by brute-force backtracking (`sheaf.global_sections`), not by cohomology.

- **"The dimension of $H^{0}$ = ker d … is bounded below by the number of admissible global sections in the discrete sense."** (§2.3) → **false, and contradicted by the table.** `water_compact + exchange_table` has $\dim H^0 = 132$ and **512** discrete global sections; $132 \geq 512$ is false. The 512 one-hot section vectors all live in $\ker d$, so at most $\dim H^0=132$ of them are linearly independent — i.e. $H^0$ is an *upper* bound on the number of *independent* sections and says nothing like a lower bound on the *count*. The direction of the inequality is simply wrong.

- **"The empirical content of the proposition is the basin volume reported in §5."** (§6, Prop. 2) → **overstated.** The basin volumes are not empirical. They are a deterministic consequence of eight hand-placed Gaussian bump centers (`attractor_sweep.fitness`) of fixed width 0.35; §7 itself concedes "no estimation from any dataset." Calling a hand-tuned bump volume "empirical content" two sections later is an internal contradiction.

- **"Capitalism's stability is a geometric coincidence … wins $3.1\%$ of the parameter cube."** (Abstract; §5) → **supported as arithmetic, but circular as inference.** The 3.1% is reproduced exactly by the code. But it follows tautologically from the choice to place the capitalist peak at an extreme corner `(0.15, 0.9, 0.9, 0.9)` while placing neo-feudal `(0.65,0.25,0.4,0.4)` and state-command `(0.55,0.75,0.55,0.85)` nearer the cube's interior. A corner peak is truncated by the boundary and loses grid cells to more centrally-peaked competitors *by construction*. "Capitalism is rare" is an input, not a finding.

- **"the basin volumes reported below are exactly $\beta$-invariant … returns identical volumes … in every case."** (§3) → **overstated.** The argmax argument is correct in principle, but `results.json` `beta_robustness` shows $\beta=20$ differs from $\beta=5/12$ at the 4th decimal (neo-feudal 0.24930 vs 0.24937; commons-fed 0.11850 vs 0.11843; state-command 0.18359 vs 0.18366), from floating-point ties near zero-fitness cells. "Exactly … identical … in every case" is literally false against the paper's own data.

- **Three-settlement results, violence slice, scenario corners** → **fully supported.** I checked every quoted decimal (16/64/32 stalks; $H^0$/$H^1$; 512 sections; 0.963→0.616→0.205→0.084→0.042; state-command 0.058→0.583→0.896→0.279; corners 99.7/95.7/99.99/96.8/99.0/99.2%; 0.683/0.315) against `results.json`. All match. This is good scientific hygiene.

- **"only the first two [repairs] are necessary … The third sits on an edge that was never the obstruction."** (Abstract; §4) → **supported and genuinely the paper's best result.** `safe_conduct` operates on edge $AC$, which is in $E\setminus E_b$, and the section count stays 0/512 with or without it.

## Argument

- **§2.3 / §4 — the cohomology is ornamental.** Everything the paper actually *uses* runs off two elementary objects: the brute-force section enumerator, and $E_b$ = the set of edges whose endpoint stalks have empty restricted intersection (Lemma). Both are pure CSP/set theory; neither needs sheaves, cohomology, or $\mathbb{R}$-coefficients. The Lemma's proof is correct but elementary. The $H^1$ numbers are reported and then never drive a single conclusion.

- **§2.3 — the residual $H^1=1$ is misinterpreted.** The paper frames a non-zero $H^1$ as "the repair has reduced the obstruction without yet eliminating it." But the $H^1=1$ in the **512-section** configuration is the topological $H^1$ of the underlying triangle (first Betti number 1 of the cycle $A$–$B$–$C$), not an institutional obstruction — there is no obstruction left, the order exists. The paper never explains the persistent 1, and its "obstruction" gloss is wrong for exactly the cases where the order glues.

- **The machinery is motivated but never exercised.** The genuine reason to use sheaf cohomology over plain CSP is the case where every *pairwise* overlap is satisfiable yet *no global* section exists (a cyclic/contextuality obstruction — the Abramsky–Brandenburger phenomenon the paper name-drops). The worked example never produces such a case: in every row, "all pairwise intersections non-empty" coincides with "global section exists." The paper builds a cathedral and only ever uses the doorbell.

- **§5/§6 — the dynamics layer is decoupled from the sheaf and from data.** The eight fitness bumps are independent of the seven-dimensional institutional state space; the attractor labels are attached to bump locations by authorial fiat. Nothing in §5 is constrained by §2–§4. The "basin geometry" is therefore a restatement of the modeler's priors, and Proposition 2's quantitative claim inherits that circularity.

- **Genuinely strong:** the safe-conduct/$E_b$ "repair-on-a-glued-edge-is-theatre" diagnostic is a real, transferable insight; the reproducibility discipline (every number traceable to `results.json`) is exemplary; §7 is an unusually candid limitations section.

## Factual errors

- **§1: "The Western Han bureaucratic state gave way to the Three Kingdoms warlords."** Wrong dynasty. The Three Kingdoms (220 CE) followed the collapse of the **Eastern (Later) Han** (25–220 CE). The Western/Former Han (202 BCE–9 CE) ended with Wang Mang four centuries earlier. **High confidence.**
- **§3: Lewis (2009) "under Sui and Tang"** — scope error as above; *China Between Empires* does not cover the Tang. **Medium confidence.**
- **§1: "the trade fairs of Champagne were 5 centuries away when the last Roman aqueduct in Gaul ran dry."** Rhetorical and imprecise but roughly defensible (aqueduct failure ~6th–7th c.; Champagne fairs ~12th c.); not clearly wrong. **Flag only.**
- No fabricated dates, figures, or technical definitions in the topology found; the cell-complex/cochain/coboundary definitions are stated correctly.

## Prose and insight

Alive: the opening ("A civilization is rarely planned and never built whole"), the catalogue of local coordination problems ("who owns the river bank, who enforces a debt, who travels armed… who is permitted to read"), and the "diplomatic effort on edges that are not the obstruction is theatre" line in §4 — that sentence is the paper. The Hanseatic-League-as-protocol and Carolingian-renaissance-as-failed-gluing readings (§2.1) are crisp and earn their place.

Dead: §3's attractor catalogue reads as a competent encyclopedia entry, eight paragraphs of "X appears when Y," and the historical examples are decorative rather than load-bearing (none constrains a parameter). §5's corner-by-corner tour ("At $(v,t,e,l)=\ldots$ the X attractor wins Y%") is a guided walk through numbers the reader cannot independently weight, since the bump centers that generate them are arbitrary.

The genuine contribution, stripped of ornament: a clean, computable diagnostic that ranks institutional repairs by whether they touch an actually-blocking interface ($E_b$), demonstrated on a transparent toy. That is real and useful. It does not require — and the paper does not actually use — sheaf cohomology, and it is oversold as both a cohomological result and a quantitative finding about capitalism's rarity.

## Top fixes

1. **Resolve the $H^1$ contradiction or demote cohomology to motivation.** Either (a) exhibit a worked example where all pairwise overlaps are non-empty yet no global section exists, so $H^1$ (or $\mathbb{Z}$-torsion) detects something CSP cannot — this is the only thing that justifies the apparatus — or (b) honestly reframe the paper around $E_b$ + section enumeration and present cohomology as suggestive future scaffolding. As written, $H^1$ is claimed to measure obstruction while the data show it doesn't.

2. **Fix the false $H^0$ bound.** §2.3's "$\dim H^0$ is bounded below by the number of admissible global sections" is contradicted by your own table (132 vs 512). State the correct relation: discrete sections lie in $\ker d$, so their *linear span* has dimension $\leq \dim H^0$; the *count* can be exponentially larger.

3. **Explain the residual $H^1=1$.** Identify it as the topological $H^1$ of the cyclic complex and stop calling it a leftover institutional obstruction in the configurations that admit 512 sections.

4. **Demote "empirical content" and "geometric coincidence."** Relabel the 3.1% as what it is: a property of the chosen bump placement and width, not a finding. Show the basin volumes' sensitivity to bump *center* and *width* (not just $\beta$, which is provably inert), so the reader can see how much of "capitalism is rare" survives reasonable re-specification.

5. **Correct the Western Han → Eastern Han error** (§1) and the Lewis Sui/**Tang** scope (§3); soften the Greif "credit predates banking" gloss to his actual contract-enforcement claim and note it is contested.

6. **Fix the §3 $\beta$-invariance overstatement** to "argmax-invariant in principle; basin volumes invariant to floating-point at the 4th decimal," consistent with the `beta_20` row.

7. **Repair the §4 factorization wording.** "$B$ has $2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2\cdot2=32$ residual configurations once its money is conditioned on the choice $C$ makes" is self-contradictory (conditioned money is fixed → 16, not 32). The correct bookkeeping is $A(4)\times B(16)\times C(4)\times M_{\text{shared}}(2)=512$; state it that way.

8. **Connect the two layers, or say plainly they are independent.** Right now the sheaf (§2–§4) and the attractor sweep (§5) share notation but nothing else. Either let the consistent global sections of §4 seed or constrain the attractor fitness, or state in §3 that the dynamics layer is an unconnected companion model so readers don't infer a coupling that isn't there.
